SHIFA Mental Health
and Disability Project

  • Shifa works with marginalized and vulnerable communities of the Ghaad* area in the Saharanpur district, Western Uttar Pradesh, North India. This area borders the districts of Haryana, Uttarakhand, and Himachal Pradesh.

    • (* Ghaad means that it is high elevated area situation near Shivalik hills. The population is predominantly Muslim. In this region Gujjars are found in both Hindu and Muslims and there are large number of schedule caste villages. Land here is sandy which is low fertile for agriculture. Weather here is extreme. Very hot in summers and very cold in winters having below average rainfall. The community is semi-migrant, highly dependent on multiple sources of livelihood sources. Due to its bordering region the development work is slow and haphazard.)

  • PROMOTING AND PREVENTING MENTAL HEALTH AND RESILINECE BY ENHANCING LOCAL CAPACITIES AND RESOURCES AND BY STABLISHING MEANINGFUL AND GAINIFUL CONNECTION, THEREBY IMPROVING THE QUALITY OF LIFE!
